# Customer Segmentation Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing customer data and segmenting customers into different groups based on their behavior. The goal is to help businesses understand their customers better and improve marketing strategies.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement
Businesses often struggle to target the right customers. By segmenting customers based on their income and spending behavior, companies can create personalized marketing strategies and increase revenue.

---

## 📂 Dataset Information
- Dataset includes:
  - Customer ID
  - Age
  - Gender
  - Annual Income
  - Spending Score
- Source: Kaggle / Sample dataset

---

## 🧹 Data Cleaning
- Checked for missing values
- Removed duplicates
- Verified data types
- Handled outliers (if any)

---

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)
- Analyzed distribution of age, income, and spending score
- Visualized relationships:
  - Income vs Spending Score
  - Age vs Spending Behavior
- Identified patterns in customer behavior

---

## 🤖 Model Used: K-Means Clustering
- Applied K-Means algorithm to segment customers
- Used **Elbow Method** to find optimal number of clusters
- Grouped customers into meaningful segments

---

## 📈 Results & Insights
- Identified different types of customers:
  - High Income, High Spending → Premium Customers
  - High Income, Low Spending → Careful Customers
  - Low Income, High Spending → Target Customers
  - Low Income, Low Spending → Budget Customers

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations
- Focus premium offers on high-spending customers
- Provide discounts to increase engagement of low spenders
- Use targeted marketing campaigns for each segment

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies
- Python (Pandas, NumPy)
- Matplotlib / Seaborn
- Scikit-learn

---

## 📌 Conclusion
Customer segmentation helps businesses make data-driven decisions and improve customer targeting strategies, leading to better business growth.
